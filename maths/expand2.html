Multiply into Bracket (algebra)

Expand A(Bx + C) into \(Dx + E\)

Guide:

- Expand the brackets step by step:
- Take the outer term (A) and multiply with each of the terms in the bracket (Bx & C), like this: A(Bx) + A(C)
  - Remember to retain any (-) negative signs! - Lastly, multiply to get the result: \(Dx + E\).
E.g.: 3(4x + 5)
3(4x) + 3(5)
= \(12x + 15\)

Negative numbers:
\(-A(Bx - C)\)
= \(-A(Bx) - A(-C)\)
= \(-A(Bx) + (-1)(A)(-1)(C)\)
= \(-A(Bx) + (-1)(-1)(A)(C)\)
= \(-A(Bx) + (+1)(AC)\)
= \(-ABx + AC\)

E.g.: -2(4x + 5)
-2(4x) - 2(5)
= \(-8x - 10\)

E.g.: -4(2x - 3)
-4(2x) - 4(-3)
= \(-8x + 12\)
